How it works

Step-by-step rehearsal

Each step builds on the last. Move through the store physically — this is a live walkthrough, not a desk exercise.

01
🚶

Walk the zones

Guide your partner through each store zone as if leading a real customer — from entrance to checkout.

02
✦

Share the story

In each zone, share key information and highlight the storytelling cues that make the moment memorable.

03
💬

Reflect together

After the rehearsal, each team member shares their favourite part of the tour and why they love presenting it.

04
🌟

Refine & inspire

Encourage feedback and tips. Make the tour more immersive with each round — no two tours need to be identical.

Storytelling toolkit

Lines that open doors

Use these cues as a starting point — then make them your own. The best tours feel personal, not scripted.

At the entrance
"Is this your first time experiencing The Wonder Walk?"

Opens without pressure. Invites curiosity rather than triggering a defensive response.

On the collection
"This piece was inspired by Daniel Swarovski's original vision — precision catching light."

Connects product to heritage. Turns an object into a story the client feels part of.

On gifting
"Is this a treat for yourself, or for someone who deserves to feel special?"

Warm, non-transactional. Creates space for the client to share — which reveals exactly how to serve them.

At the farewell
"Enjoy it — it suits you beautifully."

The last word becomes the memory. Use the client's name here. This is the moment that travels with them.

After the rehearsal

Reflect as a team

Take five minutes together. No pressure — just honest conversation.

What was your favourite zone to present — and what made it feel alive when you said it out loud?

Was there a moment where the tour genuinely felt like more than information — where it felt like an experience?

What's one thing your partner did during their tour that you want to borrow for your own?

If a customer only remembered one thing from the tour, what would you want it to be?
